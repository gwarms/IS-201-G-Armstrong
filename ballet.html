Ballet is not just something you watch or dance, it is something you live. The very nature of dance is to embody it in every aspect of your life.

I started dancing ballet at the age of ten and I continue dancing with BYU's Theatre Ballet Company today.

My favorite ballet to perform in is the Nutcracker. Below are some photos from when I last performed in the Nutcracker.

Ballet teaches you a lot about yourself and life. Some of the lessons I've learned include:

  1. Work Ethic
    • When you're dancing for hours every day, you have to learn when and how to work hard and when to conserve your energy so you can make the most out of it.
  2. Time Management
    • When you've committed to 4+ hours every day of class and you have rehearsal hours on top of that, you learn quickly how to manage your time so you can still work and do school.
  3. Teamwork
    • No show can run without everybody working together. THe role of a man is to partner the lead women. You have to put in many hours of practice to develop the coordination and trust between yourself and your partner before you go on stage.

Of course, I could never hope to compare to the skill level of the greats at The Royal Ballet in London.
